Model Reduction in Reactive Transport
Ben McLaughlin

Reactive transport systems in subsurface aquifers can be mathematically represented by a system of differential equations. Numerically solving these systems in multiple dimensions using traditional methods can be computationally cost-prohibitive, particularly in applications, such as parameter estimation, where many realizations of the forward model are required. We seek to reduce the computational cost of repeatedly solving the forward model while maintaining the reliability of the results. With this goal in mind, we explore the application of reduced order modeling, via proper orthogonal decomposition, to reactive transport systems.
